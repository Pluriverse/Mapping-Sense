MAPPING SENSE

REFERENCES

GROUND: Establish rituals that will nurture you on your sens/e making journey. These will provide safety and distance from your research when necessary. Return to the consent/s, ethics and values contained in your proposal and allow these to guide you when you lose your way.
(Figure 1)

1

GATHER: Arrange your texts, gain an overview of the expansive body of work that has transpired.
(Figure 2)

2

LISTEN: Sit in the thick presence of what you have collected, trace your internal response. Be with the living data and let meanings emerge. Remember ‘nothing is sterile’ (Haraway, 2016, p. 64).
(Figure 3)

3

MAP: Choose a consistent method of documentation. Map key learnings, relevant relationships, connections, metaphors, and symbols (Manders & Chilton, 2011, p.8)... identify gaps.
(Figure 4)

4

BRACKET: Ask the question, what am I projecting onto the data? Explore personal assumptions and beliefs, ‘work against existing or taken-for-granted theories in order to see the data in new ways’ (Kaptain, 2018, p.223), bracket what you think you know.
(Figure 5)

5

BREATHE: Settle in and ask ‘what can we deal with?’ (Green, 2020). Identify the areas of your data you feel most connected to, stay here.
(Figure 6)

6

AMPLIFY: Respond to what is emerging, tease it out and utilise the arts to communicate. Amplify what is ‘seen, felt, and tacitly known in the experience of viewing artworks’ (Kapitan, 2018, p.225).
(Figure 7)

7

DISTILL: Welcome the influence of new texts, apply aesthetic reflexivity, literature, discussion, art making (Gerber & Meyers-Coffman, 2017). Allow space for reinterpretation, transformation and distillation. Be present to what is emerging, the essence of the ‘thing’.
(Figure 8)

8

DIALOGUE: Phenomenological interaction, two people/things/beings trying to understand each other's experience (Kapitan 2018, p.198). The process is relational and deeply intersubjective, back and forth, revealing and revealing (Green, 2022b).
(Figure 9)

9

WITNESS: Open yourself up and give yourself time to be with what remains, remember ‘results are valid when they conform to the reality and narration of the life world they reflect’ (Kapitan, 2018, p.201).
(Figure 10)
